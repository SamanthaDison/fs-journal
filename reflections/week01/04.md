## Questions

1. What is the purpose of wire-framing? 
The purpose of wire-framing is to plan and mock out a layout for a webpage prior to building code. This helps to provide a clear plan for the design and where to begin formatting containers, rows, and columns.

2. What are the different levels of detail in wire-frames and why is each one useful?
    * Low-Fidelity: low-detail mock-up to outline basic structure and layout; good for a quick brainstorm and to provide a 'skeleton' structure' to work off of and present to clients
    * Mid-Fidelity: more accurate representation of the layout including more detailed components, typography, headings, etc; good for providing a more actualized concept of design
    * High-Fidelity: fully detailed mock-up of website design and should display the "final" design; these are useful for providing a clear copy to emulate in code and reduces any additional design choices to be made while actually writing code.

3. Do you think wire-frames are worth the Time, Energy, and Effort that they require? Why or Why not?
Yes absolutely! Wire-framing is a key part of the web design process. Having a clear plan before building code will help with organizing and save time throughout the coding process. Without wireframing, the coding and design processs is occurring simltaneously, this can lead to further complications down the row when making small adjustments and add time to the overall development process.

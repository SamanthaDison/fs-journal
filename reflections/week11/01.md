## Questions

1. What does Inheritance accomplish for us in C#?
Inheritance allows us to extend a class from another class, these are called base classes (the one being passed) and derived classes (the one that inherits another class).

2. How does Member inheritance work in C#? Does a class inherit all members of the base class?
A class will inherit all members of the base class except for the constructor and contingent upon their access modifiers. For example; any private classes will be protected and not inherited (unless otherwise nested in a parent class). There are also internal and protected that will affect inheritance.

3. How does accessibility affect inheritance?
(See above as well) Access modifiers affect which members may be derived i.e., private, internal, and protected classes will affect inheritance.

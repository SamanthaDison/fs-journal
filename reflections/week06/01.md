## Questions

1. What is Component based architecture?
A method of encapsulating individual pieces of a user interface into independent systems (components).

2. What are some benefits of Component based architecture?
Components behave independently and have their own interfaces. They make their own requests to servers and update without affecting other components or the UI. Components contain all methods and APIs withing themselves making it easier to navigate and more legible. Components are also reusable.

3. What are some drawbacks to Component based architecture?
CBA's can become over-indulgent if every aspect of the UI is composed entirely of components. Too many components can also lead to a decrease in readability.

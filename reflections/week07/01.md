## Questions

1. What is Pair Programming? What are the roles and how do they work?
Pair programming is when two developers share the same workspace for a single project. There is the driver and navigator; the driver is the one responsible for writing code and the navigator focuses on overall direction and assits.

2. What are some benefits of Pair Programming?
Some benefits include having an active partner to troubleshoot code with and bounce ideas off of; you may be able to find solutions quicker with another set of eyes. Another advantage is being able to swap between driving and navigating if one partner starts to experience 'brain-mush.'

3. What are some drawbacks to Pair Programming?
Some drawbacks may be if one partner is more 'competent' and essentially hogs the workspace leading to hostility and defeats the collaboration purpose.
